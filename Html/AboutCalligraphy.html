Arabic Calligraphy Styles

Kufic Calligraphy

Kufic calligraphy is one of the oldest forms of Arabic script, known for its angular, geometric shapes. It is often used in architectural decorations and early manuscripts.

Naskh Calligraphy

Naskh is a more fluid and legible style compared to Kufic. It is widely used for writing books and documents due to its clarity and ease of reading.

Diwani Calligraphy

Diwani is known for its intricate, decorative style, often used in official documents and ornate design. It features flowing curves and elegant, tightly connected letters.

Thuluth Calligraphy

Thuluth calligraphy is distinguished by its large, elegant letters with sweeping curves. It is often used in architectural inscriptions and decorative pieces.

Ruq'ah Calligraphy

Ruq'ah is a modern style of Arabic script known for its simplicity and ease of use. It is commonly used for everyday writing and informal contexts.

Jali Diwani Calligraphy

Jali Diwani is a variant of Diwani with more elaborate and intricate forms, used for decorative purposes and in official documents.

Maghribi Calligraphy

Maghribi script is known for its distinctive, rounded letters and is primarily used in North Africa. It has a unique visual appeal and historical significance.

Farsi Calligraphy

farsi script is known for its distinctive, rounded letters and is primarily used in North Africa. It has a unique visual appeal and historical significance.

Oriental Calligraphy Styles

Kaishu (Regular Script)

Kaishu is the most legible and standardized script used in daily writing. It features clear, well-defined strokes and consistent spacing. Often the foundation for learning other calligraphic styles, it combines simplicity with readability, making it ideal for both formal and informal contexts. It is widely used in books, newspapers, and documents. Mastery of Kaishu provides a solid base for exploring more stylized scripts.

Xingshu Running Script

This style allows for quicker writing and more expressive, personal touches. It is commonly used for personal notes and artistic expressions. Caoshu (Cursive Script)

Caoshu, or Cursive Script, is known for its highly stylized and expressive strokes. The characters are often abbreviated and fluid, emphasizing artistic freedom over legibility. Its used for personal expression and creative works, allowing for a wide range of brushstrokes and forms. Caoshu's abstract and energetic style makes it less formal and more artistic. It reflects the calligraphers mood and personality

Lishu (Clerkly Script)

Lishu, or Clerkly Script, features compact, angular strokes that are easy to write quickly. Historically used for official documents and records, it is valued for its clarity and structure. Although less fluid than other scripts, Lishus precision and formality make it suitable for administrative and historical texts. Its distinctive style bridges the gap between practicality and tradition.

Zhuanshu (Seal Script)

Zhuanshu, or Seal Script, is an ancient and intricate style often used for seals and official stamps. It is characterized by its ornate, blocky shapes and historical significance. This script has a distinctive, formal appearance, with each character meticulously crafted. It is used mainly for artistic and ceremonial purposes, reflecting the grandeur of traditional Chinese calligraphy

Shuishu (Brush Script)

Shuishu, or Brush Script, is a modern style that combines traditional calligraphic techniques with a more free-form, expressive approach. It is known for its spontaneous brushstrokes and artistic flair, suitable for personal and decorative writing. Jinshe (Gold Script)

Jinshe, or Gold Script, is a rare and decorative style known for its use of gold ink or gold leaf. It is often used for ceremonial or high-status documents and artworks. The script features elaborate, luxurious designs that highlight the prestige and elegance of the written word. Gold Script adds a touch of opulence to calligraphy, making it suitable for special occasions and artistic displays.
